# Decide Activity — Section 1: Scope and Applicability

## Round 1

### Candidate Voted On: "Risk-Proportionate Scope with Quick Screening"

These guidelines apply to any procurement decision where the product or service uses machine learning, neural networks, or statistical models trained on data to generate outputs that influence organisational decisions or operations. This includes direct AI procurement, embedded AI features within larger products, and AI-as-a-service through APIs or cloud platforms.

To determine the appropriate level of assessment, procurement teams complete a five-question screening tool. Based on the answers, the procurement is classified as requiring standard assessment (full evaluation against risk categories) or streamlined assessment (vendor disclosure review and basic documentation only). The screening tool does not require AI expertise to complete.

Vendors selling to consortium members are expected to disclose AI/ML capabilities in their products and to complete a standardised AI disclosure form upon request. Contracts should include provisions for notification when AI capabilities are materially changed post-purchase. Vendor self-certification against recognised AI standards (where available) is encouraged but does not replace buyer assessment.

Personal productivity tools selected by individual employees are out of scope and should be addressed through acceptable use policies.
